*(My last name is pronounced /upadʰje/ but /upaðje/ or oo-pa-the-ye also works)*

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Psychology at Tufts University, where I am affiliated with [NeuroCognition of Language Lab](https://kuperberglab.com/). If I had to choose a hat, it would be that of a cognitive scientist interested in how language is represented, processed, and generated by humans and artificial systems. Much of my work leverages psycholinguistic theory, computational modeling, natural language processing, and behavioral experimentation in service of these questions.

Previously, I completed my PhD in [Language Science](https://www.langsci.uci.edu/about.php) at the University of California - Irvine, where I was advised by Dr. Richard Futrell and affiliated with the Language Processing Group and the meaning lab.
Prior to that, I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Cognitive Science with a concentation in Machine Learning and Neural Computation and a minor in Math from the University of California - San Diego. I also spent a gap year in industry, where I worked on signal processing for optical sensing across a range of R&D applications.
